Best Courses About EV's

  • By Meenakshi Shankar
  • May 28, 2020

The technology associated to Electric Vehicles has been a new arena to me but because it is also an emerging field it’s quite difficult to find detailed information from the internet once you start digging for very specific designs and calculations for the different components. That’s where the courses offered by various training organisations/ online learning platforms helps you get a more broad perception on how to approach the design you are looking for and even get an opportunity to interact with the many experts in the field. Here are a few courses I attended:

1. EV_Technology Course offered by Devise Electronics. Pvt Ltd

This course has been an amazing journey and helped me grasp an in-depth idea of the different design aspects and simulation possibilities available to proceed with. The course gives sessions for each and every basic component required for an EV. Moreover one could expect to find many professionals working in start-up companies of the same field attending the courses and hence it’s a great opportunity to network and seek guidance.

2.Solar Vehicle Design and Development Course by ISIE India

This was more of a course that could give you more insight into what is expected out of the EVSC panel in a more competition point of view. It’s a course where you could expect more of pointers to watch out for as highlighted in the Competitions Handbook. Alongside covering the concepts in a brief manner, the course offers you insights as to how some of the previous participants approached and adapted new innovations on their EV designs to improve the performance.

3.Introduction to Battery Management System (offered by University of Colorado Boulder)

Being a part of the BMS team, I really found the courses under this specialization available in Coursera to be one of the most detailed one available out there. It covers the in-depth understanding and working of the different sub-units within a BMS. Aligning more towards the theoretical portion these courses could help instil in anyone interested how at the grass root level different electronics concepts are incorporated to make the complete battery management systems for your battery packs. I am still engaged with these courses and looking forward to the simulation aspects for making SOC/SOH estimations and the also the Battery Balancing methodologies.
